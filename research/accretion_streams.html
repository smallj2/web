The Alignment of Circumbinary Disks Around Binary Star Systems

A disk around one component of a binary star system with sufficiently high inclination can undergo Kozaiâ€“Lidov (KL) oscillations during which the disk inclination and disk eccentricity are exchanged. Previous studies show that without a source of accretion, KL unstable disks exhibit damped oscillations, due to viscous dissipation, that leave the disk stable near or below the critical inclination for KL oscillations. With three-dimensional hydrodynamical simulations I show that a highly misaligned circumbinary disk that flows onto the binary components forms highly inclined circumstellar disks around each component.

We show that a continuous infall of highly inclined material allows the KL oscillations to continue. The circumstellar disks form at a higher inclination than the initial circumbinary disk inclination (top panel). The circumprimary disk (solid line) undergo KL cycles, since the inclinations are above the critical angle, where the disk inlcination and eccentricity oscillate (panels 1 and 2). The lower three panels show the evolution of the longitude of the ascending node, disk mass, and mass accretion rate.

The KL disk oscillations produce shocks and eccentricity growth in the circumstellar disks that affect the conditions for planet formation Fu et al. (2017). .

The alignment process of a cirucmbinary disk may affect how planets form around binary star systems.
