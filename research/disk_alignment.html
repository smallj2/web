The Alignment of Circumbinary Disks Around Binary Star Systems

Accretion disks around binary star systems (circumbinary disks) are ubiquitous in the galaxy, and planet formation is thought to transpire within these disks. Circumbinary disks are regularly observed to be misaligned with respect to the binary orbital plane Czekala et al. (2019). A misaligned circumbinary disk will eventually evolve to a stable orientation, either co-planar or polar (i.e., 90 deg.) with the binary orbital plane.
