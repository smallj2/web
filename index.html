Welcome!

My name is Jeremy Smallwood. I'm currently the Dodge Family Prize Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Oklahoma.


I am a computational astrophysicist specializing in advanced simulations to explore planet formation and interpret astronomical observations. My research focuses on the dynamics of circumbinary disks around binary star systems, the interaction between gas and dust in highly misaligned circumbinary disk configurations, the impact of unbound perturbers (such as stellar flybys) on protoplanetary disks, and the N-body dynamics governing planetary systems.

Curriculum Vitae

Research

My research encompasses a range of topics, including the dynamics of protoplanetary and accretion disks, planet habitability, fast radio bursts, and white dwarf pollution. My primary focus is on understanding the evolution of gaseous protoplanetary disks in single, binary, and higher-order star systems. Using a combination of computer simulations and analytical calculations, I investigate planet formation around binary star systems, which are prevalent in our galaxy. In particular, my work explores the challenges of detecting planets within misaligned protoplanetary disks, highlighting the potential for undiscovered planets with non-coplanar orbits.

Disk Alignment

Misaligned circumbinary disks align to either coplanar or polar to the binary star orbital plane.

Accretion Streams

Material from a circumbinary disk flows inward, replenishing the smaller circumstellar disks.

Fast Radio Bursts

The origin of fast radio bursts (FRBs) remains a mystery. One possible mechanism for producing FRBs is the accretion of asteroids onto the surface of a neutron star.

Stellar Flyby

Stars on unbound orbits can interact with protoplanetary disks, creating disk substructures that may help explain observations.

Dusty Circumbinary Disks

Polar-aligning circumbinary disks create dust "traffic jams," which may provide a favorable environment for the formation of polar circumbinary planets.

Planetary Secular Resonances

Secular resonances between asteroids and giant planets can perturb asteroids, sending them into planet-crossing orbits that may play a crucial role in delivering key ingredients essential for planetary habitability.

Synthetic Observations

Dust rings in misaligned circumbinary disks are promising targets for ALMA, as well as upcoming observations from ngVLA and SKA.

Circumtriple Planets

The observed circumtriple disk structure around GW Ori may be explained by the presence of the first-ever detected circumtriple planet.

White Dwarf Pollution

The atmospheres of white dwarf stars are continuously polluted by metals, likely sourced from asteroids perturbed by nearby planets.
